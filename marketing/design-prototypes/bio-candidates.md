## Candidate 4 — "scale flip"


- **Characters:** 132
- **Voice rationale:** Cultural deadpan — frames the product as a domestic event ("your cousins will lose at"), then qualifies the mechanic with "deeply incorrect" instead of "terrible." "Deeply incorrect" is the register: clinical word, comic effect.

## Recommendation

**Ship Candidate 1.** "Somehow this works" is already the brief's anti-saas anchor — using it cements voice on day one and gives early followers a phrase to repeat. Candidate 3 is the runner-up; rotate it in week 4 when the audience knows the tone and you can lead with the roast.

## What was rejected (for the file)

- *"the bollywood-tollywood quiz that ruins family group chats"* — leans on a meme structure ("ruins X") that's already stale on IG.
- *"a quiz where the plots are sus"* — Gen-Z slang ages out fast and clashes with the cinematic register.
- *"plots so bad they need subtitles"* — cute but doesn't actually describe the product to a cold visitor.
- Anything starting with "the official" — sounds corporate. The OFFICIALLY BAD stamp does that job in the visuals already.
