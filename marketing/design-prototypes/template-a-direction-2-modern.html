Direction 2 — Modern Desi Poster

Restrained ornament. Single hero detail. Typography does the heavy lifting. Cleaner hierarchy, wider negative space, more "Penguin Modern Classics meets Bollywood" than "haveli wall."

Choices

One ornament, not many: a thin gold rule under the masthead and a thin gold rule above the footer, each broken by a single small mehendi medallion. Plus a single hero detail — a 280px minar/arch silhouette anchored bottom-right at 80% opacity. That's the entire ornament budget. Type carries the room: the plot is set in 72px Playfair italic with a 240px decorative gold quote mark, ranged left, like a magazine pull-quote. The accent ("Punjab") flips into --card-bw for color punctuation. Glow: one large diffuse BW halo, off-centered, blurred to 40px so it reads as light, not paint.

Type

Industry kicker in DM Sans 15px, tracked +0.55em, with thin rules either side — that's the "BOLLYWOOD" wordmark. Era + difficulty in DM Sans 12px tracked +0.4em. Plot in Playfair italic 72px (well above the 24px floor). Footer CTA in Playfair italic 32px. Watermark + edition stamps in DM Sans 11–13px tracked.

Why ship this

It reads in 1.5 seconds at 270px grid thumbnail because the plot text is huge and ranged left. It feels editorial, not chaotic. It scales: this same template works for every card in the deck without re-comping. Risk: less obviously "desi" — the cultural identity rides on the gold + flame palette and the single arch silhouette. If we lose those, it tips into generic editorial. Compensate with strong masthead microcopy and the OFFICIALLY BAD edition stamp.
