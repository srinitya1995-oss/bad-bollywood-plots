# Inspiration audit — Day 1 scan

> Lightweight pass over the 31 images in `/inspiration/`. Sampled 7 directly, inferred the rest from filename clustering + visual rhyme. This is not the full keep/replace H1 audit (that's Day 5). This sets the visual brief direction for Template A explorations.

## What the folder collectively points at

The dominant register is **warm illustrated maximalism** with a strong lean toward Pakistani/Indian contemporary illustration — chai gatherings, women in jewel-toned saris reading in courtyards, mehendi-patterned hands holding chai glasses, Rajasthani jharokha arches as compositional frames. The palette repeats: **terracotta, marigold orange, mustard, deep mauve, dusty rose, fresh green, dark teal**. There's almost no pure black anywhere — most "dark" tones land at warm brown or aubergine. This is exactly the desi-haveli emotional terrain `--bg #1A0F0A` and `--card-bw #E85D3A` were chosen to live inside.

A second cluster is **graphic poster work** — collage-cuts of classic-cinema actresses against bold flat-orange backgrounds with hand-drawn type ("I HUNT MISOGYNY"), and arched portrait illustrations with theatrical sunglasses ("BOY BYE"). These are hand-set, attitude-forward, posterised images that rely on a **single hero detail + one big typographic statement** — a perfect rhyme for Template A Direction 2 (Modern Desi Poster). They also confirm that gold + flame work *very* well as a duo when the supporting palette stays warm.

A third, smaller cluster is **atmospheric illustration** — a Parisian-style cutaway scene in cool blues and rain — that's clearly an outlier in palette, but useful as a structural reference: layered windows, multiple stories visible at once. That layering instinct could feed the carousel cover (Template B) more than the single-card Template A.

## What's strong

- **Hands, fabric, ornament textures** dominate over faces. Good — the brief asks designers to crop to hands, henna, textiles, architecture. The folder is already biased that way.
- **Theatrical attitude** in the figurative posters ("BOY BYE", "I HUNT MISOGYNY") matches the card voice register exactly: dry, cinematic, slightly unhinged. Confirms we should not soften the tone in design.
- **Warm illustrated palettes** validate the token choices. There's nothing here to argue we should add cool tones or saffron-only patriotic palettes.
- **Jharokha arch silhouettes** appear repeatedly as compositional containers — the brief's "Direction 1 = arch dominant" lands cleanly on existing references.

## What's missing (for IG specifically)

- **No 1080×1350 aspect-ratio references.** Every image is closer to 4:5 illustration or 9:16 phone wallpaper. We need to sample real IG carousels (meme accounts, film accounts) to ground how Template A reads at grid scale alongside other tiles. Day 2 task: pull 6 reference IG profiles into a screenshot grid for moodboard H2.
- **No Reel covers / motion freezeframes.** The folder is static. Reels are 20% of the planned feed. We need to source frame-grabs from desi narration accounts and dramatic-narrator film accounts (Day 4 task per brief §7).
- **No type-only references.** All references are image-led. We don't have a single example of pure-typography poster work in the folder (Bauhaus desi wedding cards, Penguin India spine treatments, Rohit Bhargava typography studies). Direction 2 (Modern Desi Poster) is more typographic than the folder currently teaches us, so we should add 3-4 type-only refs before locking that direction.
- **No Tollywood-side references.** Folder skews Bollywood / North Indian aesthetic. For TW variants we'll need temple-architecture references, kalamkari motifs, jasmine + emerald palette cues. Day 3 task when we build the TW variant.
- **No "ugly-good" references.** The folder is curated and pretty. Some of the funniest IG card accounts (think `@perfectlycutscreams` or filmi meme pages) are intentionally rough. Worth adding 2 references at the rough end of the spectrum so we don't over-polish — the cards are *bad* on purpose, the design shouldn't feel precious.

## Verdict for Day 2

The folder is strong enough to drive Direction 1 (Maximalist Haveli) confidently — every visual cue (arch, mehendi medallion, paisley flank, gold filigree) has a reference. Direction 2 (Modern Desi Poster) is partially supported (the BOY BYE poster proves the typographic treatment can land in this universe), but we should brief Day 5's H2 moodboard to specifically pull in 4 typography-led references and 4 Tollywood-side references so the deck is balanced before we scale templates.

If the user picks Direction 2 on Day 2, we backfill type references *before* refining to ship-ready, not after. If the user picks Direction 1, the folder already has us covered and Day 2 is just refinement.
