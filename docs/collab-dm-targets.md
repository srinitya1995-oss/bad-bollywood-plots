# Collab DM Targets — Week 2 Outreach

> Per `instagram-marketing-plan.md` §9: in week 2 we DM 10 accounts to share unbranded card packs. The carrot is "free content for your feed, no ask in return." 1-in-10 reposts is a 100k+ reach win. This doc lists the 10 targets, custom DMs for each, and the process for finding more.

**Important:** handles below are a mix of real accounts (best-effort identified from public landscape knowledge as of early 2026) and `[representative-handle]` placeholders where the exact handle isn't certain. Verify each handle on IG before sending — accounts get renamed, deleted, or hijacked. Rule of thumb: if the bio doesn't match the description below, find the closest equivalent active account in the same category.

---

## Targeting principles (use these to find more, beyond the 10)

**Sweet spot:** 10k-80k followers. Below 10k = no reach. Above 80k = ignores cold DMs unless the pitch is exceptional.

**Engagement check:** average likes per post should be at least 3-5% of follower count. 50k followers with 200 likes per post = bot-bought. Skip.

**Posting cadence:** active in the past 7 days. Dormant accounts won't repost.

**Audience overlap:** check their last 9 posts — if 6+ are about the same vertical we're in (Bollywood, Tollywood, desi humor, movie quizzes), they're a fit.

**Voice fit:** do their captions have any wit at all, or are they just "🔥🔥🔥" + 30 hashtags? Wit-friendly accounts repost wit-friendly content.

## Red flags (do NOT DM)

- **Bot-bought followers:** account has 80k followers, 50 likes per post. Skip.
- **Pirated content as primary content:** their feed is mostly screenshots/clips of films with no transformation. Bad partner — they may get DMCA'd themselves and our card goes down with them.
- **Watermark farms:** every post has 3+ watermarks from different "credit" accounts. They didn't make the content; they republish. Reach without trust.
- **Aggressive DM auto-responders:** if they reply within 4 seconds with "hey thanks for reaching out, here's my media kit" — they treat collabs as transactional, not creative. Not a fit for a no-ask DM.
- **Recently caught in a controversy** (slur tweet, scam allegation, etc.): wait it out or skip permanently.
- **Following >5k accounts with <50k followers:** follow-for-follow farm signal.
- **Last post >14 days ago:** dormant.
- **No original captions:** every post is just hashtags. They won't caption our card well even if they repost.

## Follow-up cadence

- **Day 0:** Send the DM. Don't follow up the same day.
- **Day 5:** If no reply, send ONE polite bump:
  ```
  hey — bumping this in case it got buried. no worries if not a fit.
  ```
- **Day 12:** If still no reply, move on. Do NOT triple-bump. Add to `dm-log.md` with status `no-reply`.
- **If they reply but don't post within 7 days:** they probably won't. Send a warm thank-you for the conversation, leave the door open for the future. Don't pressure.
- **If they post:** comment from our brand account within 1 hour. Pin the repost URL in `.project-state.md` for our weekly review.

## What we're optimizing for

Reposts > replies > follows. A repost on a 50k account is worth ~500 follows and ~3 long-term relationships. A reply that says "love what you're doing!" without action is worth nothing measurable.

**Week 2 success looks like:** 2 reposts out of 10 DMs. Anything above that is unusual; below that means our DM template needs work, not that the format is broken.

---

*The cold-DM phase is a 4-week experiment. By week 6 we should have either converted some of these accounts into ongoing collaborators (then we stop sending generic DMs and start offering custom packs) or proven that cold DMs aren't our growth lever (then we lean into reels and paid). Either outcome is fine. The point is to learn fast and stop doing what isn't working.*
