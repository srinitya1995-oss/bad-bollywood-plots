## 1. The one-sentence rule

**Sound like a drily amused friend texting about a movie at 1am, not a Buzzfeed intern.**

If the card reads like a listicle headline, it's wrong. If it reads like a literary review, it's also wrong. The register is **gossip with commitment**.

## 2. What you laugh at (reference register)

Anchored in the 20-Q interview:

- **AIB roast-essay** — literary density, sharp at the system not the person
- **Always Sunny** — dark, chaotic, characters committed to bad ideas
- **Vir Das + Biswa Kalyan Rath** — political observation + deadpan storytelling
- **30 Rock + Parks & Rec** — joke density + warm ensemble
- **Groucho one-liner** — compression over soliloquy
- **Crime Master Gogo** — faux-menace, committed-to-bit
- **Circuit's poetry (Munna Bhai)** — Bombay tapori elevated to faux-literary
- **Andaz Apna Apna deadpan-spy-thriller** — characters committed to the wrong genre
- **Athadu / Pelli Choopulu / Adhurs** — underrated Telugu: hitman faking family, arranged-marriage food truck, dual-role horror-comedy

**Common thread:** characters fully committed to the wrong register. Drama played as mundane. Mundane elevated to drama. Genre confusion. Earnestness in the wrong context.

## 3. What you hate (banned register)

- **Hera Pheri** canon — overquoted, yuck
- **"Flower nahi fire hai"** — cringe, only ironic never earnest
- **Govinda double-meaning songs** — dated
- **Rishta aunty / "beta kya karte ho"** — tired except in specific hands
- **White men doing comedy** — instant zone out (Seinfeld, Louis CK, mainstream US standup)
- **Kapil Sharma register** — loud family-friendly, too warm
- **Single big punchline** structure — you want micro-laughs scattered
- **All-caps rant** — you text one deadpan line instead

## 4. Registers to USE

Ranked by fit for Nitya's voice:

### 4A. Whispered drag (primary)
One plain-English observation that drags the movie from an unexpected angle. Deadpan, not angry. The voice of a drunk witty friend summarizing at 1am.

- *"Empire's future decided by one middle-manager with a sword."* (Baahubali)
- *"Don's sister wants a respectable groom. All available grooms have fake IDs and ulcers."* (Welcome)
- *"Three engineering students invent a way to get their professor's pants off."* (3 Idiots)

### 4B. Committed-to-the-bit genre confusion
Describe the film as if everyone in it is in a different movie than they think.

- *"Two idiots think they're in a spy thriller. The villain thinks so too."* (Andaz Apna Apna)
- *"Ravi Teja plays a father and a son who are both convinced they're the hero."* (Kick 2)

### 4C. Job-description deadpan
Reduce the high-stakes plot to a LinkedIn job description.

- *"Hitman takes a three-week sabbatical as someone else's brother. Performs admirably."* (Athadu)
- *"Waiter meets assistant producer. Logistics of a catering business ensue."* (Pelli Choopulu)

### 4D. Side-character promotion
Make a minor element the star.

- *"Mom's helicopter is the real main character."* (K3G)
- *"A parrot delivers a commencement speech."* (3 Idiots — existing rubric, but lower priority than 4A-4C)

### 4E. Middle-management drama (close cousin of 4A)
Treat epic conflict as workplace friction.

- *"Cousins fight for CEO position. One has better PR."* (Baahubali)
- *"Eldest son passed over for promotion. Takes it personally."* (Mahabharata gag)

## 5. Signature moves

### 5A. The plain-English put-down (family-safe)
A direct observation in English that deflates the character without being coarse. The product is played at Diwali dinners and sangeets; the voice is dry, not edgy.

Your natural put-down vocabulary — cleaned up for the page:
- *"Takes himself very seriously."*
- *"Is not a fun uncle."*
- *"Has opinions about how everyone eats rice."*
- *"Does not approve of modern music."*
- *"Will not be joining the party."*
- *"Has a complicated relationship with joy."*

Apply: instead of *"Patriarch imposes traditional values on his liberal sons"* → *"Dad takes himself very seriously about everything."*

**Rule:** no slurs, no scatology, no sexual innuendo, no regional / religious / caste stereotyping. "Buzzfeed listicle" is out on one end, "adult roast comedy" is out on the other. The register is **drily amused aunt, not drunk cousin**.

### 5B. The soft-drag adjective
One adjective that's factually correct but tonally deflating.

- *"Unemployed man, unimpressed woman, food truck."* (Pelli Choopulu — "unimpressed" is the kill)
- *"Middle-class indignities, full detail."*

### 5C. The deadpan aside
Parenthetical or period-clause that undercuts.

- *"Inherits a land dispute. Also a village deity. It's a whole thing."* (Kantara)
- *"Son comes home for Diwali. Dad cries for three hours. Standard."* (K3G)

### 5D. The texting cadence
Short clauses. Periods not commas. No caps emphasis. One beat of air between sentences.

- *"Dockyard. Gold bar. Two brothers. Mom has a favorite."* (Deewaar — see §7 for why this specific phrasing over the rubric's version)

## 6. Forbidden

In addition to §3:

- "Heartwarming" / "journey" / "discovers" / "bonds" / "finds love" — generic Hollywood summary words
- Direct meme quotes in the clue ("Utha le re baba", "Mogambo khush hua") — these go in verdict lines, never clues
- Twitter-reaction syntax ("THIS movie? EVERYTHING.")
- Academic film-crit vocabulary ("meditation on class", "subversion of the form")
- Emoji
- Exclamation marks
- Overt sentimentality

## 8. Round 3 generator prompt insert

Paste this into `scripts/generate.js` as the `VOICE_FINGERPRINT` prompt block (replace the generic voice rubric for Round 3+):

---

```
VOICE: You are writing plot clues for a desi party trivia game. The voice
is specific:

- Sound like a drily amused friend texting about a movie at 1am
- Max 2 sentences, no exclamation marks, no emoji
- Use periods not commas to chunk the beats (texting cadence)
- Pick ONE of these registers per card (don't mix):
  * Whispered drag — one observation that drags the movie from an angle
  * Genre confusion — characters committed to the wrong genre
  * Job-description deadpan — reduce the stakes to a LinkedIn bullet
  * Side-character promotion — make a minor element the main character
  * Middle-management drama — treat epic conflict as workplace friction

FORBIDDEN (auto-reject):
- "heartwarming", "journey", "discovers", "bonds", "finds love",
  "meditation on", "subversion of", "redemptive", "wholesome"
- Any Hera Pheri reference or quote
- Any "Flower nahi fire hai" style quoted dialogue in the clue
- Twitter-reaction syntax ("THIS movie? EVERYTHING.")
- Single-big-punchline structure; prefer scattered micro-laughs
- Generic Bollywood-summary adjectives ("sprawling", "electrifying", "iconic")
- Academic film-critic vocabulary

ANCHOR SPECIFICITY (still required — from the voice rubric):
- Every clue must contain ONE concrete detail unique to this film
  (a specific object, profession, location, relationship, or event)
- If stripping the anchor still leaves a guessable card, the anchor
  wasn't pulling its weight. Rewrite.

EXAMPLES OF THE VOICE:
- Baahubali: "Empire's future decided by one middle-manager with a sword."
- 3 Idiots: "Three engineering students invent a way to get their
  professor's pants off."
- Welcome: "Don's sister wants a respectable groom. All available grooms
  have fake IDs and ulcers."
- Athadu: "Hitman takes a three-week sabbatical as someone else's brother.
  Performs admirably."
- Pelli Choopulu: "Unemployed man, unimpressed woman, food truck.
  Middle-class indignities, full detail."
- DDLJ: "Boy meets girl on a Eurail trip. Two hours later, pigeons are
  involved."

TEST BEFORE YOU SHIP A CARD:
1. Does it sound like a dry 1am text? (If it sounds like a film review,
   rewrite.)
2. Would Nitya laugh at this? (If it feels like Kapil Sharma or a Buzzfeed
   listicle, rewrite.)
3. Is there a specific anchor unique to this film? (If stripping one noun
   still identifies the film, the anchor is weak.)
4. Is it under 2 sentences? (If not, compress.)
```

## 9. Voice drift alarm

Any of these in the wild = voice is drifting, full-deck re-audit needed:

- Cards using "heart-warming" or "sprawling"
- Cards longer than 2 sentences
- Cards referencing Hera Pheri in the clue
- Cards quoting dialogue directly
- Cards that feel warm (Kapil-register) instead of drily amused
- Cards that feel like listicle headlines

## 10. Application gate

Round 3 and forward: every card passes voice-fingerprint check before the generic voice rubric. If a card passes the generic rubric but fails the fingerprint, it fails. The fingerprint is the hard gate.

Round 1 and 2 staged cards don't retroactively need to pass fingerprint (they're already written in the generic voice). Post-merge, a separate voice-rewrite pass can upgrade them to fingerprint voice if the correct-rate / feedback data warrants it.
