## 1. One-line pitch

A desi party trivia game where players guess Bollywood and Tollywood movies from intentionally terrible plot descriptions.

## 2. Product identity

- **Shipped name:** Bad Desi Plots (live at baddesiplots.com; legacy badbollywoodplots.com 301 redirects)
- **Internal name:** Seedha Plot
- **Version:** 2.2.0 (domain swap + card scale landed 2026-04-20)
- **Format:** React 18 + Vite + TypeScript PWA, mobile-first, installable
- **Platforms:** Any modern phone browser, installable PWA on iOS / Android, works offline after first load

## 3. Who it is for

### Primary audience
- 18–35 year old desis in India and the diaspora
- Active Bollywood and Tollywood watchers who argue about movies at every family or friend gathering
- Party game lovers already playing Codenames, Wavelength, Monikers, or similar

### Secondary audience
- Multi-generational family gatherings (Diwali, weddings, reunions) where a parent can jump in on an 80s Sholay card and a cousin knows the 2020s Vikram cards
- Desi college and grad-school groups running trivia nights

### Not the audience
- Solo players looking for a daily puzzle. Solo mode exists as a warm-up and a killing-time mode, but the product is built for groups.
- Non-desi general trivia players. The plots, cast hints, and cultural references are too specific.
- Anyone who wants accurate movie summaries. The whole joke is that the plots are bad.

## 4. Jobs to be done

When a desi group gathers and the conversation drifts into "remember that movie…", they hire Bad Desi Plots to:

1. **Start a game without a setup tax.** Open the URL, pick a mode, enter names, play. No install required, no account, no tutorial.
2. **Give everyone equal air time.** Pass & Play rotates the reader, so the quiet cousin reads a card too.
3. **Reward deep-cut knowledge without punishing casual players.** Easy cards are 1 pt, medium 2, hard 3. A streak bonus rewards the person who binge-watches Hotstar.
4. **Turn an argument into a punchline.** The plot description is a setup; the answer reveal is the drop. Both sides laugh, nobody wins too hard.
5. **End cleanly.** After 12 cards in party mode, hand out a verdict and a winner. In solo, a lives-based round ends when the player misses three in a row.

## 5. Principles

1. **Phones-first.** Every screen must work at a 320px viewport. Desktop is a courtesy.
2. **Zero learning curve.** A new player at a party should understand the rules inside two cards. No tutorial overlay.
3. **Offline-first.** Card database ships with the PWA. Fire-and-forget writes to Supabase for analytics. Network failure never blocks play.
4. **Cultural authenticity, not Indian theming.** Real filmi type, real desi palette, real voice. No brown-rendered paisleys generated by a Google image search.
5. **Ship the laugh.** If a card doesn't make the reader laugh, it doesn't ship. Voice rubric in `docs/card-voice-rubric.md` is the gate.

## 7. Shipping quality bar

For any feature to leave `ship/*` and merge to `main`:

- TypeScript typecheck: zero errors
- All unit + component tests pass (186 tests as of 2026-04-30)
- Production build clean, bundle < 250KB gzipped JS
- Visual QA score >= 7.0 / 10.0 on affected screens (see `engineering-spec.md` Gate 3)
- Mobile preview (375px viewport) manually tested via Vercel preview
- Animation coherence verified on mobile (iOS + Android), desktop (Chrome + Safari)
- Reduced-motion paths verified — animations degrade to opacity-only fades without silencing state changes
- Supabase + PostHog writes are fire-and-forget and never block the game loop

## 8. Roadmap

### v2.2.0 — Card scale (next)
- 500 Bollywood + 500 Tollywood = 1000 total cards
- Per-era distribution targets (20% pre-2000, 40% 2000s, 40% 2010s+)
- Stricter fact-check pipeline (inline Wikipedia verification per card)
- Difficulty rebalancing using real play data from v2.1 analytics

### v2.3.0 — Social mechanics
- Shareable round summary card (image export of Results screen for WhatsApp / Instagram)
- Daily card (one card everyone gets that day, so group chats can argue the same plot)
- Rivalry tracking across sessions (Rahul vs Simran head-to-head, opt-in)

### v3.0.0 — Beyond movies
- Additional decks: 90s TV serials, cricket match moments, Bollywood songs, ad jingles
- Mode variants: Picture round (poster fragments), Dialogue round (infamous lines)
- Localization (Hindi + Telugu UI strings, still English as primary)

### Explicitly not building
- User accounts or login. Anyone can play.
- Ads. The product is ad-free and stays that way.
- In-game purchases. All content is free.
- Native iOS/Android apps. PWA only.
- AI-generated plot descriptions for new cards. Every card is hand-written to pass the voice rubric.

## 9. Success metrics

See `applied-scientist-spec.md` for event schema and dashboards. Headline numbers:

- **Activation:** % of visitors who complete at least one full round (12 cards party or 3-miss solo)
- **Retention D7:** % of users returning within 7 days of first round
- **Session length:** median cards played per session (target: 10+)
- **Replay rate:** % of rounds where the final CTA "Play Again" is tapped
- **Share rate:** % of rounds where the user shares the result (once v2.3 shareable card ships)
- **Party mix:** fraction of rounds with 2+ players (target: >60%)

## 10. Business model

- Free, ad-free, PWA-only
- Hosted on Vercel (cost: essentially zero at current scale)
- Supabase free tier handles analytics event writes
- No monetization planned for v2 or v3
- If adoption scales past free-tier limits, the author self-funds infra before considering any paid tier

## 11. Competitive landscape

| Product | What it does well | Where Bad Desi Plots differs |
|---|---|---|
| Codenames | Team-based word deduction, broad cultural recall | We're single-device pass-and-play, no team setup, pure movie trivia |
| Wavelength | Physical party game, subjective guessing | We're digital, objective guessing with a right answer |
| Bollywood Zone / Filmy Dairies on Instagram | Bollywood trivia content, passive | Bad Desi Plots is played, not watched |
| Trivia apps (Trivia Crack etc) | Broad trivia, solo + multiplayer | We are deep-desi, party-first |
| Random free trivia sites | Free content, weak polish | We ship with design-grade polish, mobile-first, offline-first |

## 12. Open product questions

Tracked for future sessions, not blocking v2.1.0:

- Should solo mode have a leaderboard across sessions (opt-in only)?
- Should verdicts be industry-specific (different lines for BW-heavy vs TW-heavy rounds)?
- Is there a "difficulty mode" picker (easy-only, hard-only, mixed), or is mixed the only right default?
- Do we surface the streak bonus more visibly (currently only shows in the +PTS float)?
- Should the card show the decade before the reveal, as a hint for hard cards?

## 13. What breaks this product

If any of these become true, the product stops working as intended:

- Cards become factually wrong. Trust dies the first time a user Googles an answer and finds the card was wrong. The fact-check pipeline exists to prevent this.
- Cards become boring. If the voice slips, the laugh doesn't land, and the product is just a trivia app. The voice rubric gate (`docs/card-voice-rubric.md`) exists to prevent this.
- Setup friction grows. Every modal, tutorial, or account requirement pushes the time-to-first-card up and the party-won't-play rate up.
- Mobile layout breaks. A single iOS Safari bug at 375px width is a full-product outage for our audience.
- Load time crosses 3 seconds on a mid-tier 2021 Android. PWA offline-first is what keeps the app feeling native.

These are the things the engineering gates and visual QA pipelines defend against.
