## Section A — Reference & canon

**Q1.** Rank "this is me" across AIB roast-essay / Vir Das deadpan / Biswa storytelling / 30 Rock joke density / Always Sunny chaos.
**A1.** All five. No ranking; they all apply.
**Reveals.** Chaos and joke-density register is in scope, not just dry deadpan. The existing fingerprint under-weights Always Sunny chaos and over-weights aunt-level restraint. Full reference basket stays live.

**Q2.** One movie line you've actually quoted at a party this year.
**A2.** "Ghar ki yaad nahi aayi Jassi?"
**Reveals.** Comfort with family-arc lines used drily in non-family contexts. The quoted register is a flat deadpan check-in, not a performance line. Good anchor for the voice.

**Q3.** Funniest Andaz Apna Apna character?
**A3.** Amar and Crime Master Gogo.
**Reveals.** Confirms fingerprint §4B (committed-to-the-bit genre confusion) and the Gogo register (faux-menace, committed-to-bit). The two characters together are the register target: a clueless hero plus a loud villain both taking the wrong genre seriously.

**Q4.** Are Hera Pheri and Munna Bhai both dead on the clue side?
**A4.** Dead. Both.
**Reveals.** Tighter than the fingerprint's v2 position, which only banned Hera Pheri. Munna Bhai references now ALSO banned in clue text. Safe to quote in verdict lines only per existing §6 rule.

## Section B — Register & tone

**Q5.** Group-chat register: aunty / grandma / chaotic sibling / other?
**A5.** Sharp-tongued grandma + chaotic sibling.
**Reveals.** This is the single biggest update to the fingerprint. The "drily amused aunt" framing in §5A is too reserved. Replace with "sharp-tongued grandma who has been to every wedding" plus "chaotic sibling who commits to the bit". Grandma is the observational floor; sibling is the chaos ceiling. Both live inside the same card deck.

**Q6.** Roast rhythm: single dry line, or two-beat setup plus punch?
**A6.** Two-beat.
**Reveals.** Confirms fingerprint §5D texting cadence. Lock two-beat as the default card structure. One-beat cards are the exception, not the rule.

**Q7.** Sincerity inside a card, 0-10?
**A7.** Low. Also flagged a side rule: not too many spoilers.
**Reveals.** Sincerity close to zero. Plus a content rule that is adjacent to but not identical to the fingerprint: clues must not hand away endings. Capture as a separate gate in the rubric.

**Q8.** Can a card ever compliment the movie while describing it?
**A8.** No.
**Reveals.** Hard rule. No "perfectly paced thriller about..." No "iconic scene where..." No "beautifully shot..." Strip every complimentary adjective. Confirms and tightens fingerprint §6.

**Q9.** Funniest joke in the group, or sharpest observation?
**A9.** Both.
**Reveals.** Cards must be sharp AND land a laugh. Sharp without laugh equals film-review voice (banned). Laugh without sharpness equals Buzzfeed listicle (banned per §3). The gate is joint, not either-or.

## Section C — Taboo & edges

**Q10.** Sex, scatology, innuendo?
**A10.** Out. Rare-and-earned occasionally.
**Reveals.** Default is out. A handful of cards per 1000 can earn it if the joke is actually the edge, not just the edge as content. Maintain the "drily amused grandma, not drunk cousin" ceiling.

**Q11.** Religion jokes?
**A11.** Only systems.
**Reveals.** Systems (pandit politics, priest bureaucracy, temple-trust board drama, mosque-committee infighting, church fundraiser awkwardness) are fair game. Deities are not. This is a sharper rule than the fingerprint currently encodes.

**Q12.** Caste jokes?
**A12.** Only punch-up allowed.
**Reveals.** Never punch down. Card voice is allowed to drag dominant-caste character arrogance, not the reverse. This is a real rule, not a "we avoid caste" hedge.

**Q13.** Political figures in clues?
**A13.** Via subtweet only.
**Reveals.** Never name a living politician. Refer to archetype (the minister, the CM candidate, the independent MP, the party-office rising star) and let the reader map it. Safer legally too.

## Section D — Form & compression

**Q14.** Max card length?
**A14.** 2 sentences.
**Reveals.** Hard cap. Confirms fingerprint §5D. Any card over two sentences fails on sight.

**Q15.** Hindi or Telugu words in clues?
**A15.** No. All English.
**Reveals.** DIRECT CONTRADICTION of the existing fingerprint which tolerated rare transliterations. Update fingerprint to ban all non-English words inside the clue body. Proper nouns that are film-title canonical still allowed (e.g., "Bahubali" as the answer, "Pushpa" as a named character).

**Q16.** Lowercase + comma-style like owner's own chat messages, or keep title-case clean?
**A16.** No. No em dashes though.
**Reveals.** Keep sentence-case / title-case. Cards are product copy, not chat. The em-dash ban is global (already memory-locked as `feedback_no_em_dashes.md`).

**Q17.** Beat rhythm: one / two / three?
**A17.** Two.
**Reveals.** Confirms Q6. Two beats per card is the signature. Three beats is allowed when the third is a one-word kicker ("Standard.") per fingerprint §5C, but the base structure is two.

## Section E — Audience & self-calibration

**Q18.** Mom-test bar?
**A18.** She laughs.
**Reveals.** Higher bar than "she doesn't frown". The card has to earn a laugh from a 55+ yr-old desi woman. This kills most edge-lord humor by construction. Keep.

**Q19.** Work-Slack regret triggers?
**A19.** The full NO list (locked):
  - Anti-feminist content
  - Mean-spirited content (mean without wit)
  - Classist content
  - Casteist content
  - Bad language (exception: "shit" is allowed)
  - Rudeness to: babies, families, marriages, single people
**Reveals.** This is the hardest and most specific rule in the whole interview. It deserves its own section in the rubric. Every card auditor must have this as a scoring dimension. A single violation = card killed, not revised.

**Q20.** Men vs women as targets?
**A20.** Men as target: good, makes me chuckle. Women as target: no.
**Reveals.** Card voice punches at male characters, not female ones. Men's vanity, arrogance, incompetence, self-seriousness, ego = fair game. Women's equivalent traits = not fair game in the same voice. This asymmetry is intentional and the deck must reflect it.

**Q21.** Audience in owner's head when writing?
**A21.** All — but priority order: college gang / cousins / strangers (Reddit).
**Reveals.** Priority 1 is the college gang (shared cultural reference stack, tolerant of chaos, fast). Priority 2 is cousins (multi-generational, family-safe cutoff). Priority 3 is Reddit strangers (context-light, need anchor details). Work friends and parents do not drive the voice.
